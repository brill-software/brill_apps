Introduction

The Brill Software Proprietry License (BSPL) is aimed at preventing other companies from taking the Brill CMS and Brill Framework and providing the product directly as a service, without collaborating with us. We're reminded of the brilliant Netflix film called "The Billion Dollar Code" that dramatizes the legal battle between Terravision and Google over Google Earth. We're happy for other developers to use generic packages and modules from our source code in their own developments, should they be useful. But we also need to make a living and earn money to enable to our products to be developed further.

Large organizations can save substantial sums of money by using our "low code" approach and can therefore justify paying for licenses. For personal use, non-commercial projects and small businesses, the Brill CMS and Brill Framework are free. Hopefully some of the people using the product for free will contribute something back to the project in the way of feedback, details of bugs and ideas for enhancing the product.

Community, Enterprise and Distributor Licensing

We've copied the new Docker model for licensing our software. It's free for personal use, non-commercial projects and small businesses with fewer than 250 employees AND less than $10 million USD in annual revenue. If you quality under the free use, you come under the Community Licensing. Should you work for a large company, organisation or government department, you come under the Enterprise Licensing. The Distributor licensing is for if you wish to re-sell our products or provide a SaaS offering similar to WordPress or Wix.
